<h3 class="title">Origin</h3>
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<p>Ralphie Parker’s confrontation with neighborhood bully Scut Farkus in the classic 1983 holiday film <em>A Christmas Story</em> did not cause the TBS network to cancel their yearly marathon showing of the movie, but a social media rumor caused some viewers to fear that Christmas wouldn’t be the same without it.</p>
<p>The rumor was apparently started by a meme appearing to represent an article from “MomusFeed News,” which was mocked up to resemble the style of the digital publication <a href="https://www.buzzfeednews.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">BuzzFeed News</a>:</p>
<p><a href="https://www.snopes.com/tachyon/2018/11/christmas-story.jpg"><img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-173257 size-full" data-lazy-sizes="(max-width: 449px) 100vw, 449px" data-lazy-src="https://www.snopes.com/tachyon/2018/11/christmas-story.jpg?resize=449,454" data-lazy-srcset="https://www.snopes.com/tachyon/2018/11/christmas-story.jpg?w=449 449w, https://www.snopes.com/tachyon/2018/11/christmas-story.jpg?resize=80,80 80w" data-recalc-dims="1" src="https://www.snopes.com/content/themes/snopes/dist/images/lazyload-placeholder.png"/><noscript><img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-173257 size-full" data-recalc-dims="1" sizes="(max-width: 449px) 100vw, 449px" src="https://www.snopes.com/tachyon/2018/11/christmas-story.jpg?resize=449,454" srcset="https://www.snopes.com/tachyon/2018/11/christmas-story.jpg?w=449 449w, https://www.snopes.com/tachyon/2018/11/christmas-story.jpg?resize=80,80 80w"/></noscript></a></p>
<p>We confirmed with a TBS representative that the network would not canceling their holiday marathon airing of the beloved movie. The rumor appeared to be nothing more than a social media hoax.</p><div class="creative">
<div id="post-body-1">

<p>In the film, which is set in the 1940s, a grown Ralphie Park narrates his memory of a Christmas from his childhood, which included the antics of his little brother Randy and his parents. <em>A Christmas Story</em> has become an enduring cinematic favorite, likely due to its quirky comedy and memorable scenes, one of the most famous of which depicts Ralphie’s hapless encounter with a beleaguered mall Santa.</p>
<p></p><center><iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/IQM_kWxrJNI" width="560"></iframe></center>
<p>Throughout the film, adolescent bully Scut Farkus and his sidekick Grover Dill torment the neighborhood children, which culminates in Ralphie’s tackling him and giving him a bloody nose. In 2016, a now-grown Zack Ward, the actor who portrayed Farkus, gave a lighthearted take on how to “bully proof” a child for the holidays:</p>
<p></p><center><iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/PdWQGY7dE1g" width="560"></iframe></center>
<p>Fans of the movie will get to watch Ralphie’s father obsess over his prize fishnet stocking-adorned <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lR29zxrY2LM" rel="noopener" target="_blank">leg lamp</a> starting at 7 P.M. Central Time on Christmas Eve on TBS. The movie marathon will run throughout Christmas day.</p>
